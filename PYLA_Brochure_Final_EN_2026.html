03 · The Framework

What is PYLA and what does it produce?

Applied cognitive intelligence. Not a psychological test. Not a diagnosis. A direction tool.

PYLA is a cognitive direction framework that combines perceptual observation, metacognition, processing styles, and pattern recognition to generate practical information about how a person thinks, learns, decides, and operates.

Through a 100% visual experience — no complex forms, no introspective questions — PYLA identifies the cognitive profile and translates it into concrete direction: for individuals, teams, and organizations.

"PYLA does not diagnose or label. It observes patterns, identifies tendencies, and transforms cognitive data into practical direction."

04 · Corporate Context

Five talent challenges PYLA can help organizations address.

PYLA does not solve these problems directly. It provides cognitive information that, incorporated into concrete decisions, can help improve the related factors.

01

Turnover in the first 90 days

An underestimated cause of early attrition is the mismatch between a collaborator's cognitive profile and the actual demands of the role. PYLA can help identify that incongruence before it results in departure.

→ Useful information for: selection, onboarding, 30/60/90-day follow-up

02

Generic onboarding without cognitive adaptation

Integration processes treat everyone the same. Knowing a new collaborator's cognitive profile allows the leader to adapt how they accompany them — not the role content, but how to communicate and structure it.

→ Useful information for: induction, mentor assignment, leader communication

03

Team dysfunction without structural diagnosis

Teams with strong individual talent and low collective performance. Communication conflicts are frequently unidentified cognitive differences. PYLA makes that map visible before it becomes burnout.

→ Useful information for: team development, conflict resolution, reorganization

04

Promotions without leadership profile information

The best technician gets promoted and becomes the worst manager. PYLA can inform whether a collaborator's cognitive profile is congruent with a higher-responsibility role — before the decision is made.

→ Useful information for: potential assessment, career planning

05

Training without cognitive segmentation

Development programs are generic by design. PYLA enables identifying groups with similar cognitive profiles to make development more relevant — increasing the likelihood that the format and content resonate with each person.

→ Useful information for: L&D program design, mentoring, individual development plans

09 · Educational Sector

Five educational challenges PYLA can help institutions address.

The information PYLA generates, incorporated into guidance and support processes, can contribute to improving factors related to retention, vocational clarity, and student development.

First-year dropout due to incongruent program choice

PYLA applied at induction identifies students with high profile–program incongruence before it manifests as low performance or dropout. Information for preventive intervention.

Program change in the first two semesters

Prior guidance was insufficient. PYLA offers a fast, visual, and validated alternative to complement the guidance process — before entry and in the first semester.

Vocational support without structural data

Counselors work with interviews and observation, but without objective cognitive information. PYLA gives them a concrete instrument to structure and prioritize support.

Cohort profiling without a standardized tool

Retention teams operate reactively because they lack early information about their cohorts. PYLA generates the group cognitive map in hours — not months.

Specialization decisions without self-knowledge

In the second and third year, students choose specializations without information about their real cognitive strengths. PYLA can support that decision with concrete profile data.
